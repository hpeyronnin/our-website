Expanding electricity access: Where can private sector participation make the biggest impact?

To achieve UN Sustainable Development Goal 7, which aims to ensure universal access to clean, affordable electricity, private investment should target countries with low access to electricity and high investment potential.

This chart uses data from the 2017 Bloomberg New Energy Finance ClimateScope index, which ranks emerging markets based on attractiveness for clean energy investment, and 2014 World Bank data for electricity access.

Which countries stand out?

  • India

    • In India, 269.5 million people lack access to electricity, about 25% of the world total.




  • Nigeria

    • In Nigeria, 74.7 million people lack access to electricity, about 7% of the world total.




  • Ethiopia

    • In Ethiopia, 70.8 million people lack access to electricity, about 6.7% of the world total.

  • Bangladesh

    • In Bangladesh, 59.9 million people lack access to electricity, 5.7 % of the world total.

  • Tanzania

    • In Tanzania, 44.1 million people lack access to electricity, about 4.2% of the world total.




  • Uganda

    • In Uganda, 30.9 million people lack access to electricity, about 2.9% of the world total.
